Pancakes

prep: 5min | cook: 10min | serves: 2

You will need:
  • 1 cup of oats
  • 1/2 cup of milk (we love it with oat milk, but it's your choice)
  • 1 ripe banana
  • pinch of salt (small pinch)
  • vanilla essence (if you have it)
  • maple syrup (if you have it)
    topping options for serving:
  • honey
  • yoghurt
  • jam / marmalade
  • nutella
  • berries and/or fruit
How to make it:
  • - Put all of the ingredients into a blender or food processor and combine until it forms a smooth batter.
  • - Place a spoonfull of batter into a pre-heated and lightly oiled frying pan.
  • - Flip the pancake after a couple minutes/once it's lightly browned. Repeat on the other side.
  • - Serve with your choice of toppings.

Pizza

prep: 25min | cook: 10-15min | serves: 3

You will need:
    base:
  • 200g self-raising flour
  • 200g natural yoghurt (plant alternatives are as good!
  • tsp baking powder
  • pinch of salt
    sauce:
  • 1/2 tin tomatoes
  • herbs of your choice (dried basil, italian seasoning)
  • 1 clove of garlic, crushed
  • pinch of salt & pepper
    toppings:
  • mozzarella cheese (or vegan alternative)
  • options: pepperoni, jellapeno peppers, mushrooms
How to make it:
    sauce:
  • - Before you start, turn on your oven to 200 fan/220 degrees & put your pizza dish in!
  • - Pour all of the sauce ingredients into a saucepan and cook it for 5 minutes.
  • - Take the pan off the hob and set aside. It is ready to go on to the pizza base later.
    base:
  • - Combine all of the base ingredients in a bow by using clean hands. Kid's love to get involved in this part so allow them to help you!
  • - Keep on combining it until you form a smooth dough.
  • - When you think it's done, put your formed dough-ball onto a lightly floured surface and kneed it for 2-3 min and let it rest for 10 min.
  • - Now it's time to prepare your pizza-toppings & salad if you are having one!
    finale:
  • - Form a round shaped flat pizza base (or the shape of your baking tray!).
  • - When you're happy with how it looks, put it onto the tray.
  • - Top it with sauce you've prepared earlier.
  • - Lay cheese on top and any other ingredients of your choice
  • - Put it into the oven for 10-15 min and enjoy!

Chickpea curry

prep: 10min | cook: 35min | serves: 4

You will need:
  • 1 large onion
  • 2 large ripe tomatoes (or 400g tin tomatoes)
  • 1 fresh chilli pepper
  • 3 tbs fresh corriander
  • tsb grated ginger
  • 400g coconut milk
  • 400g chickpeas (cooked)
  • a wedge of lemon
  • rice for serving
    spices:
  • tsp turmeric
  • 1/2 tsp chilli powder
  • tsp garam masala
How to make it:
  • - Chop: onion, tomatoes, chillies, coriander, ginger and set a side.
  • - Use a large saucepan to heat a tablespoon of oil (I use groundnut oil for this curry but you can you whatever you have).
  • - Tip in your chopped onion and cook it for about 5min on a medium heat stirring regularly. Don't allow it to change colour.
  • - Mix all of your spices in a small bowl with a splash of water, then scoop it into the pan with onions.
  • - Once the seasonings coated the onions and are toasted, toss your tomatoes, ginger and chillies into the pan.
  • - Allow to cook for 2-3min then pour in enough water to just cover the contents.
  • - Season with a pinch of salt. Stir regularly!
  • - Pour coconut milk, bring it to simmer and then tip your chickpeas into the pan.
  • - Bring it all to simmer and reduce the heat. Cook for futher 15-20min.
  • - Stir in chopped coriander once the heat is switched off.
  • - Serve on a bed of boiled rice and squeeze lemon juice on top.
