We would like to share a few recipes you could be be learning at one of our workshops.
Before you ask, let me reassure you that we change our recipes every four weeks so you can keep on learning how to cook new meals every month.

Pancakes

prep-time: 5min | cook-time: 10min | serves: 2

You will need:
  • 1 cup of oats
  • 1/2 cup of milk (we love it with oat milk, but it's your choice)
  • 1 ripe banana
  • pinch of salt (small pinch)
  • vanilla essence (if you have it)
  • maple syrup (if you have it)
  • serving options:
  • honey
  • yoghurt
  • jam / marmalade
  • nutella
  • berries and/or fruit
How to make it:
  • Put all of the ingredients into a blender or a food processor and combine until it forms a smooth batter.
  • Place a spoonfull of the batter into a preheated and lightly oiled frying pan.
  • Once the batter firms up and is lighlty bworned (2-3 min), flip the pancake. Repeat on the other side.
  • Serve with your choice of toppings.

Pizza

prep-time: 25min | cook-time: 10-15min | serves: 3

You will need:
  • base:
  • 200g self-raising flour
  • 200g natural yoghurt (plant alternatives are as good!
  • tsp baking powder
  • pinch of salt
  • sauce:
  • 1/2 tin tomatoes
  • herbs of your choice (dried basil, italian seasoning)
  • 1 clove of garlic, crushed
  • pinch of salt & pepper
  • toppings:
  • mozzarella cheese (or vegan alternative)
  • options: pepperoni, jellapeno peppers, mushrooms
How to make it:
  • sauce:
  • Before you start, turn on your oven to 200 fan/220 degrees & put your pizza dish in!
  • Pour all of the sauce ingredients into a saucepan and cook for 5 minutes.
  • Take the pan off the hob and set aside. It is ready to go on to the pizza base later.
  • base:
  • Combine all of the base ingredients in a bow by using clean hands. Kid's love to get involved in this part so allow them to help you!
  • Keep on combining the ingredients until they form a smooth dough.
  • When you think it's done, put your formed dough ball onto a lightly floured surface and kneed it for 2-3 min then let it rest for 10 min.
  • Now it's time to prepare your pizza-toppings (& salad if you are having one)!
  • finale:
  • Form a round shaped flat pizza base (or the shape of your baking tray).
  • When you're happy with how it looks, put it onto the tray.
  • Top it with sauce you've prepared earlier.
  • Lay cheese on top and other ingredients of your choice
  • Put it into the oven for 10-15 min and enjoy!

chickpea curry

prep: 10min | cook: 35min | serves: 4

You will need:
  • 1 large onion
  • 2 large ripe tomatoes (or 400g tin tomatoes)
  • 1 fresh chilli pepper
  • 3 tbs fresh corriander
  • tbs grated ginger
  • 400g coconut milk
  • 400g chickpeas (cooked)
  • a wedge of lemon
  • rice for serving
  • spices:
  • tsp turmeric
  • 1/2 tsp chilli powder
  • tsp garam masala
How to make it:
  • Chop: onion, tomatoes, chillies, coriander and ginger. Set a side.
  • Use a large saucepan to heat a tablespoon of oil (I use groundnut oil for this curry but any cooking oil works well).
  • Tip your chopped onion into a saucepan and cook it for about 5min on a medium heat stirring regularly. Don't allow it to change colour.
  • Mix all of your spices in a small bowl with a splash of water. Scoop it into the pan with onions.
  • Once the spices coat the onions and are lightly toasted, toss your tomatoes, ginger and chillies into the saucepan.
  • Allow to cook for 2-3min, then pour in enough water to just cover the contents.
  • Season with a pinch of salt. Stir regularly!
  • Pour coconut milk, bring it to simmer and then tip your chickpeas into the saucepan.
  • Bring it all to simmer and reduce the heat. Cook for futher 15-20min.
  • Stir in chopped coriander once the heat is switched off.
  • Serve on a bed of boiled rice and squeeze lemon juice on top.
